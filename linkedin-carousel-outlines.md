3. **Paw Care:** Waterlogged streets breed fungal infections between paw pads. Dry paws thoroughly after every walk. Check between toes. Coconut oil helps prevent cracking.

6. **Timing Strategy:** Walk during rain breaks, not during downpours. Mumbai monsoon follows patterns — early morning often has lighter rain. Check the radar before heading out.
7. **Don't Skip Walks Entirely:** Dogs need exercise year-round. Skipping walks for 4 months creates behavioural and health problems. Adapt the walk — shorter duration, covered routes — but don't stop.

## 8. Understanding Your Dog's Body Language During Walks

**Slides:**

1. **Hook:** 7 Things Your Dog Is Telling You on Every Walk — Are you listening?
2. **Tail Position:** High and wagging ≠ always happy. A stiff, high wag often means arousal or tension. A relaxed, mid-level wag with whole-body movement = genuine happiness.
3. **Ears Forward vs Back:** Ears forward = alert, curious, engaged. Ears flattened = stressed, fearful, or submissive. Ears that keep shifting = processing multiple stimuli.
4. **Pulling Forward:** Could mean excitement, prey drive, or anxiety to escape. Context matters — pulling toward another dog is different from pulling away from a loud noise.
5. **Lagging Behind:** Not stubbornness. Could be fatigue, pain, fear of something ahead, or overstimulation. If your dog suddenly slows down, check in — don't just tug the leash.
6. **Stress Signals:** Lip licking, yawning, whale eye (showing whites), excessive panting when it's not hot. These are calming signals — your dog is saying "I'm uncomfortable."
7. **The Happy Walk:** Loose body, soft eyes, relaxed mouth (slightly open), easy gait, occasional sniffing. This is what every walk should look like.

3. **Walk Before 7 AM or After 6:30 PM:** The 10 AM to 4 PM window is off-limits. Period. No exceptions. Not even "quick" walks. The risk isn't worth it.
